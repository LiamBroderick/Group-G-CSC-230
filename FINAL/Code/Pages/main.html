Who Are We?

Glazer Children’s Museum is Tampa’s very own interactive, learning laboratory where children play, discover, and connect to the world around them to develop as lifelong learners and leaders. Here at Glazer Children’s Museum, we want to build a community that values PLAY as the foundation for the learning and development of children and families across generations!

We at Glazer Children’s Museum believe that play is for everyone. We are committed to providing a place where all families can come together to play, discover, and connect with the world around them. When people play together, they develop empathy, understand each other more, and build stronger bonds.

Why Is Child Development Important?

There are 5 domains involved regarding children development...

physical,

social emotional,

sensory,

cognitive,

communication

The exhibits here at Glazer Children’s Museum are dedicated to focus and enhance these different domains!

1. Physical - Developing motor skills helps children understand how their bodies interact with the world around them, fostering exploration and physical confidence.

2. Social/Emotional - Supporting social and emotional development teaches children to recognize and manage their feelings, understand others' emotions, and build positive relationships, fostering empathy and cooperation.

3. Sensory - Sensory development is vital for children as it helps them explore and understand their surroundings, laying the groundwork for learning, communication, and cognitive growth.

4. Cognitive - Cognition also involves the mental activities of understanding information and processes, organizing, remembering, and applying problem solving and generalization to situations.

5. Communication - Communication skills are essential for children's ability to express themselves, understand others, and engage with the world around them, laying the groundwork for social interaction and learning.

Why Is Child Led Play Important?

When children are given the freedom to lead their play, they become more engaged, motivated, and invested in the activities they're involved in. This autonomy allows your child a sense of ownership and empowerment as they feel in control of their learning and exploration.

By letting children take the lead, they can follow their natural curiosity and interests, which enhances their overall learning experience. It encourages them to explore different ideas, experiment with various scenarios, and problem-solve independently. Child-led play also promotes creativity, critical thinking, and decision-making skills as children navigate through their play experiences.

This is why here at Glazer Children's Museum we encorage child led play!
