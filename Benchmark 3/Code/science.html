The Science of Play

"Children need play now more than ever. It teaches them resilience, problem solving, risk taking, and empathy. As a vital part of this community, the Glazer Children's Museum is committed to serving the children of Tampa Bay and remains steadfast in our mission to provide a safe outlet for imagination and discovery. Join us as we embrace the power of play in the next decade and beyond." - Sarah Cole, President & CEO

Here at the Glazer's Children's Museum, we create and foster engaging, impactful PLAY experiences for children and families to learn and thrive.

What Are the Types of Play?

"Play is often talked about as if it were a relief from serious learning. But for children, play is serious learning. Play is really the work of childhood." - Fred Rogers (Mister Rogers)

There are many types of play offered at the GMC and beyond that your child can engage in, each with their own strenghts in teaching critical life skills. These types of play include:

  • Symbolic/Imaginative
  • Rough and Tumble
  • Role Play
  • Communications
  • Locomotive
  • Mastery/Object/Replicative

The Principles of Play

"Play is the highest form of research." - Albert Einstein

In order to maximize the benefits your child gets from play, the GMC is specifically designed to encourage the following pillars of play:

  • Freely Chosen
  • Intrinsically Motivated
  • Self Directed
  • Process over Product
  • Meaningful
  • Ongoing
  • Not Adult Led

What Do We Learn from Play?

"Children learn as they play. More importantly, in play, children learn how to learn." - O. Fred Donaldson

While playing at the GMC and beyond, Children learn a variety of essential life skills such as:

  • Executive Function Skills (Focus, Adaptation, Moderation, Emotions)
  • Social Skills (Communication)
  • Problem Solving (Open Mindedness, Collaboration, Questioning)
  • Critical Thinking
  • Resilience
  • Self Regulation
  • 21st Century Skills
