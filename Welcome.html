Why Is Child Development Important?

There are 5 domains involved regarding children development. The exhibits here at Glazier Children’s Museum are dedicated to focus and enhance these different domains!

1. Physical - "Motor skills allow children to make purposeful movements and learn the physical characteristics of self and the environment."

2. Social/Emotional - "Emotional development includes the ability to identify and understand one's own feelings, to accurately read and comprehend emotions in others, to manage strong emotions and their expressions, to control one's own behavior, to develop empathy for others, and to establish and maintain relationships."

3. Sensory - "The ability to recognize and interpret sensory stimuli serves as the basis for understanding, learning, and knowing a particular action or reaction, which plays a major role in the physical, social-emotional, communication, and cognitive development of a child. "

4. Cognitive - "Cognition also involves the mental activities of comprehending information and the processes or acquiring, organizing, remembering, and using for problem solving and generalization to novel situations."

5. Communication - "Communication helps children learn to understand or comprehend their wants and needs."
